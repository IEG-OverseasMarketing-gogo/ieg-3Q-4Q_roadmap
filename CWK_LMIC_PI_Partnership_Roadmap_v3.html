Core Philosophy

The family is the first line of defence — and the most overlooked one

Existing clinical tools answer one question: "Does this person already have cognitive impairment?" CWK answers a different, earlier, and more important question: "How can a family recognise risk, act together, and carry the burden of care without breaking apart?"

Stage 1 · Before Diagnosis
Family
Recognition

Families are the first to notice subtle changes — forgetting names, repeating questions, social withdrawal. Yet without structured awareness tools, they dismiss these signs for years. CWK gives families a language and a framework to act early.

→
Stage 2 · Active Participation
Family
Engagement

When a diagnosis arrives, families who have been engaged from the start transition into care partners — not shocked bystanders. CWK trains family members to co-administer cognitive stimulation, reducing dependency on clinical visits.

→
Stage 3 · Sustained Care
Family
Resilience

Caregiver burnout is the hidden crisis within the dementia crisis. Families that receive structured support, peer connection, and role clarity carry significantly lower burden scores. CWK measures and actively reduces family suffering — not just patient outcomes.

"The goal is not only to treat — it is to reduce the suffering of the entire family unit. A family that recognises early, participates actively, and is supported structurally experiences fundamentally less pain."

CWK Three-Axis Mission

One kit. Three axes of impact across the full care continuum.

CWK is the only community tool designed to operate simultaneously across all three stages of the dementia journey — before clinical onset, during progression, and throughout the caregiving relationship. Each axis is measurable, evidence-grounded, and actionable without clinical infrastructure.

Axis 1 · Prevention
Early Detection
& Risk Reduction

Before symptoms appear, families carry unrecognised risk. CWK gives them a structured, WHO-aligned tool to identify modifiable factors — hypertension, social isolation, inactivity, hearing loss — and act on them before the clinical window closes.

Primary Outcome Measure
Change in WHO modifiable risk factor score (T0 → T1 → T2) per household
→ 14 modifiable WHO risk factors screened
→ Household risk score produced in 20–30 min
→ No clinical background required to administer
Axis 2 · Progression Delay
Slowing Decline
Through Engagement

When cognitive decline is underway, the family becomes the most accessible therapeutic agent. CWK trains family members to co-administer daily cognitive stimulation — memory, language, executive function — extending the reach of clinical care into the home between hospital visits.

Evidence Base
Fully immersive cognitive training: MD = 0.93 improvement in global cognitive function in MCI patients (RCT meta-analysis, 2024)
→ 3 cognitive domains per session (memory / language / executive)
→ Family-administered, no equipment needed
→ Weekly session format, 45 min, 6–12 participants
Axis 3 · Caregiver Support
Reducing Family
Burnout & Suffering

The hidden crisis within dementia is the family's crisis. Caregiver burnout is silent, cumulative, and devastating. CWK is the only community tool that actively measures and structurally addresses family suffering — not as a side note, but as a primary design goal.

Secondary Outcome Measure

Evidence base — holographic technology effect by axis

Axis 1 · Prevention
Therapeutic Effect
Evidence level: Strong

Fully immersive VR produced statistically significant improvement in global cognitive function in MCI patients (MD = 0.93, p < 0.01, meta-analysis 2024). Improvements strongest in executive function and processing speed.

CWK Hologram Application 3D risk factor body map — visualising how hypertension, inactivity, and isolation affect the brain in real time. Converts abstract numbers into visceral motivation to change behaviour.
Axis 2 · Progression Delay
Prevention Effect
Evidence level: Moderate–Strong

VR reminiscence therapy well-accepted with minimal adverse effects, high adherence, and significant reductions in BPSD (behavioural and psychological symptoms). Personalised and socially engaging formats show strongest outcomes.

CWK Hologram Application Personalised 3D reminiscence environments (childhood home, village market, traditional festivals) co-experienced by patient and family — the world's first family-unit holographic cognitive stimulation format.
Axis 3 · Caregiver
Educational Effect
Evidence level: Strong

VR/AR caregiver psychoeducation enabled family members to experience dementia from the patient's perspective (spatial disorientation, agnosia, anomia) — fundamentally changing how they interpret behaviours and reducing caregiver distress.

CWK Hologram Application "Walk in their world" — caregiver holographic empathy session. Family members experience what the patient experiences. Transforms guilt and frustration into informed, compassionate care. Directly reduces ZBI-A burnout score.
🤖
AI Data Pipeline — Phase 2 Onwards

Every field interaction — screening scores, stimulation session outcomes, ZBI-A burden indices, facilitator observations — is structured data. Pooled across 1,000+ households in two countries, this dataset enables AI-driven clustering of risk profiles by population group, personalised intervention recommendations, and the standardisation of the CWK protocol for global deployment. The physical kit is the data engine; the holographic platform is where that intelligence is delivered back to the family.

The Intervention

Cognitive Wellness Kit — four-layer community tool

The CWK prototype is already complete in English, Korean, and Russian. The AFI project funds cultural adaptation into four new languages, scientific validation across two countries, and the peer-reviewed publication that enables global scale-up.

📋

Layer 1 · Risk Awareness

Household-level WHO risk factor screening administered by trained non-clinical facilitators. Produces a modifiable risk score per individual — primary outcome measure at T0, T1, T2.

🧠

Layer 2 · Cognitive Stimulation

Culturally adapted activity cards covering memory, language, and executive function — designed for group or family use without clinical supervision.

🤝

Layer 3 · Caregiver Support

Structured caregiver burden assessment and peer-support facilitation guide. Burden index change tracked across the 12-month follow-up period (secondary outcome measure).

🎓

Layer 4 · Facilitator Certification

TVET-compatible competency framework enabling vocational institutions to certify community facilitators — the structural mechanism for zero-cost post-project scale-up.
