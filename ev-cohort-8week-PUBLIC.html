🎯 Instructor Positioning Principle
"I'm not someone who knows every possible fault. I'm someone who trains a diagnostic procedure that holds up no matter what fault you're facing."
When an unfamiliar symptom comes up → "It's not about memorizing the answer — let's narrow it down together using today's order (low-cost first → safety first → data-driven)."
🧩 6 Interactive Simulator Types (by Session)
Sequencing — complete a procedure/path by clicking steps in order (e.g. MSD 5-step)
Item selection — identify the correct item(s) among several by clicking (e.g. finding HV components)
Matching — pair concepts with their descriptions
Sorting — classify situations into buckets (e.g. act now / schedule inspection)
Meter reading — read a measured value and judge whether it's safe
Branching scenario — a roleplay where outcomes diverge based on your choices
Each session is designed around whichever type best fits that day's learning goal. E.g. the MSD procedure (Week 3) uses sequencing, warning-light triage (Week 5 D4) uses sorting, and thermal-runaway response (Week 6) uses branching scenarios.
🔀 Two-Track Strategy (4-Week Demo → 4-Week Paid)
🆓 Demo Track (Weeks 1–4, Free)
Safety + insulation diagnosis · battery diagnosis · BMS code diagnosis · charging diagnosis
Goal: prove out the diagnostic fundamentals + collect participant feedback
→
💳 Paid Track (Weeks 5–8)
Drivetrain, brake, and thermal-management diagnosis + integrated practical assessment
Goal: deepen to certification-practical level + graduation + build in-house diagnostic trainers
After the 4-week demo, incorporate simulator feedback to improve the content before opening the paid track. Be sure to mention the conversion details (price, schedule, benefits) in Week 4 Day 5.
⏱️ Daily 60-Minute Session Structure (same every day — theory notes, simulator, quiz, and written test combined)
0:00
Review quiz from last session — 5 min. One core question from the previous day. It's fine to get it wrong — this is a warm-up.
0:05
Theory notes — 15 min. Walk through 2–3 theory items for today's session + Q&A.
0:20
Interactive diagnostic simulator practice — 20 min. Internalize the diagnostic procedure through that day's dedicated simulator (sequencing/sorting/meter reading/DTC matching, etc.) + a situational comprehension-check quiz.
0:40
Written test — 10 min. 2 multiple-choice questions, judged instantly with no reference materials. Confirms whether today's theory was absorbed.
0:50
Field application discussion + preview of next session — 10 min. "How would this apply at our own shop?"
Day 5 (Friday) of every week is a "weekly integration session" that reviews that week's theory, simulator, and written test together. Keep the same 60-minute structure but increase the number of questions.
💬 Situational Scripts

Responses by participant reaction type

CASE 1 — "I already know all this" (ICE-experienced technician)

"Right, with ICE experience the concepts will click fast for you. But EV voltage levels are on a completely different scale. Today we just need to nail down that one difference."

CASE 2 — "This is too hard" (complete beginner)

"That's okay. There's only one thing to remember today: orange cover = pull the MSD before you touch it. Get just this into muscle memory and you won't have an accident."

CASE 3 — Silence / no response

"Let me ask you honestly — is there anything about working on EVs in the field right now that makes you uneasy?" (Silence is fine — just wait.)

CASE 4 — Good engagement, asking more questions

"Good question — that's actually next session's topic. Let's leave it there for today, so you'll want to come back tomorrow." (with a smile)
✅ Graduation Criteria
Demo Track Completion (as of Week 4, Day 5)
① Can perform MSD 5 steps and calculate insulation resistance thresholds on the spot ② Can classify range loss into 4 causes ③ Understands the DTC code framework and can apply first-line action procedures ④ Can classify charging failures into 4 branches and apply cross-checking ⑤ Written test average of 80+ across all sessions
Final Graduation (as of Week 8, Day 5)
① Can diagnose motor/inverter power loss across 3 cause groups ② Can distinguish normal from abnormal regenerative braking and apply the 4-step integrated brake diagnostic procedure ③ Can diagnose battery thermal management issues (overheating, leaks, pump failure) ④ Written mock exam average of 80+ ⑤ Passed both complex-fault practical scenarios

Final graduates are designated as candidates for in-house EV diagnostic trainers. This is a procedure-centered curriculum that also transfers directly to certification practical exam prep.
