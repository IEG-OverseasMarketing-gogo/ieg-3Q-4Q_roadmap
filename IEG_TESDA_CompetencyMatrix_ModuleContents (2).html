Overview

Program Summary & Equipment Overview

This document presents the Competency Mapping Matrix and Detailed Module Contents for the IEG Smart Factory TVET Program — a 32-week, 960-hour training curriculum designed to prepare BOP youth for employment in the Philippine electronics and manufacturing sector. The program is structured into two sequential phases: EIM NC II (Weeks 1–16, 480 hours) covering foundational electrical, pneumatic, and PLC competencies; and EIM NC III (Weeks 17–32, 480 hours) covering advanced PLC programming, hydraulics, industrial instrumentation, and smart factory systems. Core competencies are delivered through hands-on equipment-based training using IEG's six-product training lineup. Following TESDA's 13 July 2026 technical review, a new Part 3 — Basic & Common Competencies Enrichment Track has been added, closing the 21st Century Skills, leadership, sustainability, and entrepreneurship gaps identified in that review, entirely within the existing 32-week schedule.

Basic & Common Competencies — Enrichment Track

Added in response to TESDA's 13 July 2026 technical review letter, which confirmed strong Core Competency alignment through the equipment-based labs above, and asked IEG to also evidence Basic Competencies (21st Century Skills) and select Common Competencies — none of which are equipment-dependent. This section shows exactly where each one now sits inside the existing 32-week / 960-hour schedule.

How it works. Rather than adding new weeks or new equipment, IEG has inserted short classroom/workshop sessions directly into the existing schedule, timed to weeks where they reinforce the equipment-based lab already taking place — a Quality Standards lesson lands in the same week as instrument calibration; a Sustainability lesson lands in the pneumatic-maintenance week. Program length, cost, and equipment footprint are unchanged.
